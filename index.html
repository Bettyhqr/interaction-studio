Wind speed
Stronger winds transfer more energy to the ocean surface, creating larger and more powerful waves. The longer and more consistently the wind blows over the water, the bigger the waves grow.
Water depth
In deep water, waves with longer wavelengths move more swiftly, preserving their energy. As they reach shallower areas, their speed decreases, their height grows, and they may break as a result of friction with the seabed.
Seafloor topography
Underwater features like ridges, trenches, and continental shelves influence wave behavior. Sudden changes in depth can cause waves to refract, break, or amplify, leading to phenomena like rogue waves, tsunamis, or coastal erosion.
